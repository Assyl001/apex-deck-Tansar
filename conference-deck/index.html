The L0–L5 Framework
Six levels of autonomous investing

Level Name Behavior Human Role
L0 No Automation All analysis manual. AI tools only support. Full control
L1 Investor Assistance Single-task automation. Data collection. Alerts. Human-in-the-loop
L2 Partial Automation Integrated analysis. Sentiment + valuation. AI synthesizes. Human supervises
L3 Conditional Automation AI recommends buy/sell within defined domain. Human-on-the-loop
L4 High Automation Full autonomy within defined operational domain. Human-out-of-the-loop
L5 Full Autonomy Across all market conditions. Theoretical. Policy-only

L3 is where the institutional industry can credibly be today.

Here's how we solve them — without the architectural complexity of a generic LLM pipeline." Say: "On the left — the LLM way. A black box. Documents in. A number out. No way to trace which cells came from source versus which came from the model's imagination. This is what produces all three of the failures I just showed you. On the right — the Apex way. We decompose every DCF into roughly two hundred atomic rows. Each row is a JSON object. Each object carries three things: the value, a pointer to the exact source passage in the 10-K, and a validator that checks it. A cell that does not have a source — fails the validator. A cell that cannot be traced to a specific passage in a specific document — the pipeline stops. Not 'flags a warning.' Stops. [POINT to bottom panel] And here is the direct mapping. Hallucination — caught by the source anchor validator. A cell without a verified citation cannot pass. Corner cutting — caught by the completeness gate. If the LLM only read twelve of two hundred pages, the missing rows will not have sources. The gate blocks the pipeline before it renders an output. No normalization — caught by a dedicated validator that flags one-time items. If reported net income includes an asset sale the LLM ignored, the validator catches it before it lands in the forward projection. [PAUSE] Every failure you saw — has a specific gate that makes it technically impossible in this architecture." Transition to REI loop slide: "And here is the part I am most proud of..."
